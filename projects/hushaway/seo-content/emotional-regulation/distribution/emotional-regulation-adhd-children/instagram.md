## Caption

Your ADHD child melts down over the "wrong" breakfast cereal.

You've tried deep breathing. Calm-down jars. Counting to ten. Nothing works.

Here's what nobody told you: emotional regulation for ADHD children isn't the same as for neurotypical kids. Not slower to develop. Fundamentally different.

Neurotypical kids have a dimmer switch for emotions. ADHD kids have a light switch someone else controls. Feelings flip to full intensity instantly.

The strategies that work for neurotypical children often make things worse for your ADHD child because they fight against how the ADHD brain actually processes feelings.

Standard advice assumes your child can notice they're getting dysregulated, remember a coping strategy, execute it with focus, and wait for it to work. ADHD challenges every single one of these.

What works instead? Tools that don't require thinking, remembering, or focusing:
â€¢ Immediate sensory input (cold water, strong flavours)
â€¢ Passive sound that enters without demanding attention
â€¢ Movement BEFORE conversation (discharge the adrenaline first)
â€¢ Fewer words, more presence

Your child isn't being difficult. Their brain genuinely can't use the strategies designed for neurotypical children. You're not doing it wrong. You're just using tools that weren't designed for your child's brain.

## Visual Notes

**Carousel Design:**
- Use calming colours (soft blues, gentle greens, warm neutrals)
- High contrast text (dark text on light background or reverse)
- Clean sans-serif font, large and readable
- Minimal design elements - no sensory overwhelm
- Consistent colour scheme across all slides

**Reel B-roll Suggestions:**
- Close-up of child's hands under running water
- Child doing wall pushes or jumping
- Parent sitting quietly near dysregulated child (showing presence)
- Sound playing passively in background (speaker/device visible)
- Visual contrast between chaos and calm moments

**Avoid:**
- Showing actual meltdowns (privacy and dignity)
- Overly busy designs
- Multiple fonts or colours
- Flashing or rapid transitions
