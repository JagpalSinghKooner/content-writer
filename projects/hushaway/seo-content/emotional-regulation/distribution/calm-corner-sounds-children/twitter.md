Research shows: specific acoustic patterns influence emotional processing at a neurological level.

### Tweet 6
Not all sounds work for all moments.

RED ZONE (meltdown): Low-frequency sounds, ASMR, ambient audio
YELLOW ZONE (escalating): Predictable patterns that slow down
BLUE ZONE (withdrawn): Warm, gentle tones
GREEN ZONE (calm): Binaural beats for focus

### Tweet 7
Generic calming music often makes things worse.

Why? Unexpected key changes, complex harmonies, lyrics requiring processing.

Neurodivergent nervous systems need predictable, sensory-safe audio. Not music designed for neurotypical relaxation.

### Tweet 8
Equipment needed: At minimum, a phone or tablet with a speaker.

Better: A dedicated small speaker that stays in the calm corner.

This removes the "find the device" step during crisis.

### Tweet 9
One tip that makes a difference:

Start playing the sound BEFORE your child enters the calm corner.

Let the sound fill the space first. They arrive into an already-calming environment.

Small shift. Big difference.

### Tweet 10
For SENCOs and teachers:

In a busy classroom, headphones work best. One child can access calming sounds without disrupting others.

Create a simple protocol: When a child uses the calm corner, what sound plays?

Remove decision-making in the moment.
