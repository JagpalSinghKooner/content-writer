## Caption

Every calm corner guide tells you what objects to include.

None tell you what sounds to play.

.
.
.

Here's what we've learned: sound is the missing piece that makes everything else work.

When your child is overwhelmed, sound might be the only thing that reaches them without requiring anything in return. No choices to make. No objects to pick up. No instructions to follow.

Research shows specific acoustic patterns can influence emotional processing at a neurological level. The brainstem responds to sound before the conscious mind even registers it.

For children in the Red Zone, this matters enormously. Their conscious brain is overwhelmed. But their brainstem is still listening.

Not all sounds work for all moments. The sound that helps your child maintain focus during homework won't be the same sound that helps them recover from a meltdown.

Swipe through to learn what sounds work for Red Zone (meltdown), Yellow Zone (escalating), Blue Zone (withdrawn), and Green Zone (maintaining calm).

Equipment needed? At minimum, just a phone or tablet with a speaker. Better: a dedicated small speaker that stays in the calm corner.

One tip that makes a difference: start playing the sound BEFORE your child enters the calm corner. Let the sound fill the space first. They arrive into an already-calming environment.

Your calm corner isn't complete without sound. It's not a nice extra. It's what makes everything else work better.
