## Caption

Your child can't calm down. Not because they won't listen. Because their brain literally can't process your instructions right now.

.
.
.

Here's what nobody tells you about emotional regulation: when your child is in the middle of big feelings, the thinking part of their brain (prefrontal cortex) goes offline. The alarm system (amygdala) takes control.

This isn't a behaviour problem. It's neurology.

During this state, your child cannot follow breathing exercises, choose fidget toys, or talk about their feelings. All of those require brain functions that are temporarily unavailable.

For neurodivergent children, this happens more intensely and more frequently. ADHD brings heightened emotional intensity. Autism brings sensory overwhelm. Their nervous system is already working harder, and when it's pushed past capacity, standard advice doesn't work.

So what actually helps?

Passive support. Tools that require nothing from your child. No instructions. No choices. No demands. Just environmental changes their nervous system can respond to.

Sound is one of the most effective forms of passive support. It enters the environment without demanding anything. Your child doesn't need to engage with it. Their nervous system can respond even when their conscious brain is overwhelmed.

Understanding this changes everything. You stop blaming yourself for not having magic words. You stop blaming your child for not "just calming down." And you start looking for tools that work WITH their nervous system, not against it.

One quiet moment can change a whole day for a child. Sometimes it can change yours too.
