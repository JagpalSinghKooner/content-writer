## Caption

The rain sounds worked perfectly for bedtime. So you tried them for homework. Complete disaster.

.
.
.

Here's what nobody tells you about finding sounds for an anxious child: the same sound that works magic at 8pm can make 4pm homework time worse.

Your child's nervous system isn't in the same state all day. An anxious brain needs something different than a distracted brain. A brain overwhelmed by a transition needs something different again.

So here's how to match the right sound to the right moment:

**For anxiety:** Simple, repetitive sounds. Brown noise, gentle rain, low frequency tones. Give the brain a steady anchor without adding to the processing load.

**For focus:** White or pink noise. Binaural beats. ADHD brains crave stimulation‚Äîthe right sound satisfies that need without becoming a distraction.

**For transitions:** Gentle sounds that build slowly. Consistent cues. Have sound already playing when they walk in the door. Predictability helps the brain prepare.

**For car journeys:** Short trips need simple sounds. Long trips benefit from gentle storytelling. Both mask unpredictable road noise.

The families who make sound work build their toolkit in advance. When things are calm. When there's space to experiment.

Pick your trickiest moment this week. Match a sound to it. See what changes.

One quiet moment can change a whole day for a child. Sometimes it's just about finding the right sound for that moment.

## Visual Notes

**Carousel Design:**
- Colour palette: Soft blues, gentle greens, warm neutrals (calming, not clinical)
- Fonts: Clean, readable sans-serif. Large text for key points
- Layout: Lots of white space. Not cluttered or overwhelming
- Icons: Simple line icons for each situation type (rain drop for anxiety, book for focus, arrow for transitions, car for journeys)

**Reel B-roll Suggestions:**
- Split screen showing same child at different times of day (calm morning vs focused afternoon vs bedtime)
- Close-up of phone/speaker playing sound
- Visual of sound waves with different patterns for different situations
