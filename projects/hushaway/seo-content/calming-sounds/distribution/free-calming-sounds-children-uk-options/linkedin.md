Here's what happens when free calming sounds aren't designed for neurodivergent children:

The frequencies grate instead of soothe.
The voice jars instead of calms.
A sound that should settle actually irritates.

It's not your child being fussy. It's sensory processing differences meeting content designed for neurotypical ears.

I've tested every major free calming sound option in the UK. Here's the honest breakdown for bedtime specifically:

**Screen-free matters**
YouTube requires a device. The blue light, the visual stimulation, the temptation to tap something else. CBeebies and The Open Sanctuary can both play through speakers without your child looking at a screen.

**Consistency supports routine**
If your child finds a sound that helps them settle, being able to play it every single night without interruption (no adverts, no expired trials) reinforces the bedtime signal. YouTube adverts break this. Free trials end.

**Match the sound to the problem**
Racing thoughts respond to different audio than sensory sensitivity. Transition anxiety needs different frequencies than focus difficulties. Generic rain sounds don't address specific struggles.

For neurodivergent children specifically, here's what I've found works:

The Open Sanctuary offers frequency-based sounds (including solfeggio), ASMR textures, and nature audio designed with sensory differences in mind from the start. Not adapted from neurotypical content. Built for children who process sound differently.
