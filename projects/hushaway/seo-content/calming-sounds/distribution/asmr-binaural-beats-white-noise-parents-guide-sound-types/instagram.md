Here's why most content about these sound types doesn't help. It's either audio companies selling you something, or clinical research so dense you need a neuroscience degree to understand it. Neither tells you what to play for your child tonight.

So here's the honest picture:

**ASMR:** Research shows benefits in adults (heart rate reduction). Most studies focus on adults, not children. Safe when age-appropriate. Your child's response matters more than any generalisation.

**Binaural beats:** Mixed research. Some studies show benefits, others show no effect vs placebo. Requires headphones. Worth trying with caution. Manage expectations.

**Noise colours:** White is harsh, pink is gentler, brown is warm. All mask environmental noise. Children with sound sensitivity often prefer pink or brown.

The limitations matter. We don't have large-scale studies on long-term effects for children. We don't have neurodivergent-specific research. But that doesn't mean these sounds don't help. It means you try them with appropriate expectations and watch your child's actual response.

The search for calming sounds isn't about finding the "best" sound. It's about finding the right match for your child's brain, their sensitivities, their specific challenges.
