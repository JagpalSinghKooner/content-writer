*DR estimates from Perplexity research analysis*

### Soundsory

- **URL:** soundsory.com
- **DR Score:** 45-60
- **Angle:** Technology-mediated sensory integration combining sound with vestibular stimulation
- **Strengths:** Detailed program structure (40-day protocol), bone conduction headphones, explicit neurodivergent focus (ADHD, autism, SPD), testimonials from educators
- **Gaps:** Program-specific content only—not a comprehensive educational resource. No guidance for parents seeking to understand sound therapy generally. Requires hardware purchase.

### Safe and Sound Protocol (SSP) Providers

- **URL:** Various UK practitioners (complexconnexions.co.uk, adhdcoaching.org, move2connect.com)
- **DR Score:** 40-60 (varies by practitioner)
- **Angle:** Polyvagal Theory-based, clinical evidence (85% anxiety reduction cited), nervous system regulation
- **Strengths:** Strong scientific grounding, clinical trial data, mechanism explanation (middle ear muscles, vagus nerve)
- **Gaps:** Requires practitioner involvement—cannot self-administer. Expensive (typically £300-500+). Parents must complete protocol first. Not a resource parents can access independently.

### Harley Row Clinic

- **URL:** harleyrowclinic.co.uk
- **DR Score:** 35-50
- **Angle:** Functional medicine, "exercise for the ear," integrated with movement and nutrition
- **Strengths:** Clinical credibility, explains mechanism (auditory processing, central nervous system), assessment-based approach
- **Gaps:** Geographic limitations (London, Sussex, Surrey only). No digital or home-based options. Service-focused, not educational content for parents.

### The Calm Collective

- **URL:** thecalmcollective.org.uk
- **DR Score:** 30-45
- **Angle:** "Sound journeys" for schools and workplaces, institutional delivery
- **Strengths:** Research-backed messaging, NHS mental health stats, institutional credibility
- **Gaps:** Targets institutional decision-makers, not parents. No parent-facing educational content. Session-based, not resource-based.

### The London Sound Healer

- **URL:** thelondonsoundhealer.co.uk
- **DR Score:** 20-35
- **Angle:** Interactive sound healing and meditation for children 5-11
- **Strengths:** Clear age targeting, multi-sensory approach, references peer-reviewed research
- **Gaps:** Service-only (1-hour sessions), minimal educational content, no passive listening option—requires "fun, multisensory, interactive" engagement. Parents seeking home solutions find nothing.

### Tomatis Method / AIT Providers

- **DR Score:** 30-50 (varies)
- **Angle:** Auditory training for listening and learning improvement
- **Strengths:** Long-established methodology (1960s), some clinical adoption
- **Gaps:** CRITICALLY WEAK EVIDENCE BASE. High-quality studies show no improvement for autism. Very expensive (£1,200-2,000 for AIT). Not NDIS approved. This is the competitor most vulnerable to honest evidence-based positioning.

## 8. Key Messaging

**The market gap HushAway fills:**

Every UK competitor is either:
- **Service-based** (requires clinic visits, practitioner involvement)
- **Program-specific** (only covers their specific methodology)
- **Claims-heavy** (makes benefits claims without evidence discussion)

HushAway can be the ONLY **comprehensive educational resource** that:
1. Explains all sound therapy types honestly
2. Discusses evidence transparently (what works, what doesn't)
3. Provides practical home-based guidance
4. Requires no practitioner, no expensive equipment
5. Is designed for neurodivergent children from the start

**Trust through transparency:**

The biggest opportunity is being the ONLY resource that addresses weak evidence for Tomatis/AIT and limited evidence for binaural beats directly. Parents increasingly research claims independently. First-mover on honest positioning wins long-term trust.

**Competitor vulnerabilities:**

- Tomatis/AIT: Weak evidence base, very expensive, can be positioned against
- Soundsory: Hardware-dependent, program-specific
- SSP: Practitioner-required, expensive, not self-serve
- Wellness providers: Claims without evidence, limited credibility

**Parent questions this pillar must answer:**

1. What IS sound therapy? (Different from music therapy, meditation, etc.)
2. Does it actually work? (Honest evidence assessment)
3. Is it safe for my child? (Safety guidance, especially for binaural beats)
4. Can I do this at home? (Practical implementation)
5. What sounds should I use? (Specific guidance by use case)
6. How does this differ from what we've tried? (Passive vs active distinction)
