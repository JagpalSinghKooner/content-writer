It's 2am and you're googling "sound therapy for children" again.

Your little one still can't settle. You're exhausted. Someone on a forum mentioned sound therapy might help.

But every search gives you conflicting information. Is it music therapy? Special headphones? Those frequencies everyone talks about? Something you can try tonight, or something you need appointments for?

Here's what confused me most: "sound therapy" means completely different things depending on who you ask.

Sound therapy is an umbrella term for any approach that uses sound to support wellbeing or sensory processing. For neurodivergent children, this includes practitioner-led programmes, binaural beats, frequency-based sounds, ASMR and soundscapes, and the critical distinction between passive and active listening.

That last one changed everything for us. When your child is mid-meltdown or exhausted, they can't engage with anything demanding. Active music therapy might be brilliant at other times. But during overwhelm? They need passive listening. Press play. That's it. No tapping, no choices, no demands.

We built HushAway because we couldn't find passive, neurodivergent-first sound support that actually understood what exhausted parents need.

Sound therapy isn't magic. But for many families, it's become one useful tool among many. Something that helps with bedtime tonight. Or the car journey tomorrow. Or that post-school overwhelm.
