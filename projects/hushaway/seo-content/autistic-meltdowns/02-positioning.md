## The Unique Mechanism

**Passive listening:** Sound that works without requiring anything from parent or child.

Why passive matters during meltdowns:
- Weighted blankets require touch tolerance (often rejected during meltdown)
- Fidgets require motor control (often impossible when overwhelmed)
- Breathing exercises require cognitive load (unavailable during crisis)
- Apps require interaction (adds demands, not removes them)

**Sound bypasses all of this.** Press play. That's it.

**The mechanism explained:** Frequency-based sounds and ASMR give the overwhelmed nervous system something to land on without adding demands. The brain can't process "calm down" instructions during a meltdown—but it can receive sound passively.

## Consistency Check

**All angles share:** Sound-based regulation is the missing tool in autism meltdown support. Not as replacement for other strategies—as the passive, zero-demand option that works when other tools can't.

**Primary angle establishes:** The specificity gap. "What to actually play" is the question nobody answers.

**Supporting angles extend via:**

| Article | Extension |
|---------|-----------|
| 01 (Understanding) | WHY sound works (neurological basis) |
| 02 (What to Play) | The direct answer (most actionable) |
| 03 (After School) | WHEN to use sound (specific pattern) |
| 04 (Recovery) | How long to use sound (beyond crisis) |
| 05 (Shutdown vs Meltdown) | Which sounds for which state (nuanced) |
| 06 (Prevention) | Sound as daily tool, not just crisis |

**Potential conflicts:**

1. **Article 06 (Prevention) vs Article 02 (What to Play During):** Could seem like contradictory advice (prevent vs respond). **Resolution:** Frame prevention as reducing frequency and intensity, not eliminating meltdowns entirely. Both tools needed.

2. **Article 05 (Shutdown) could feel like scope creep:** Shutdowns aren't meltdowns. **Resolution:** Position explicitly as "the quiet version many parents miss" and acknowledge they're different experiences needing different support.

**Reader journey:**

Someone landing on ANY article should:
1. Feel seen ("You understand this")
2. Learn the mechanism ("Passive sound works because...")
3. Get specific action ("Here's what to actually play")
4. Discover more depth exists ("See our guide on [related topic]")

Whether they enter via "after school meltdowns" or "shutdown vs meltdown," they encounter the same core message: sound is the missing tool, here's exactly how to use it.
