## Caption

3:47pm. Your child walks out of school. Fine. Maybe even smiling.

15 minutes later, they're screaming on the kitchen floor.

.
.

Let's talk about what's really happening.

Your child isn't saving their worst behaviour for home. They're not punishing you. They've spent six hours holding themselves together at schoolâ€”forcing eye contact that hurts, suppressing the urge to move, hiding confusion, absorbing sensory overload. That effort has a name: **masking.**

And masking has a cost.

By the time your child walks through your door, there's nothing left. The reserves are empty. The suppression can't hold. Home isn't the problem. Home is where they finally feel safe enough to stop pretending. Everything they've been holding floods out at once.

This is masking debt.

Your child melts down at home because you're their safe person. With you, they don't have to mask. Teachers see the mask. You see what's underneath. You're not getting their worstâ€”you're getting their most honest.

After school meltdowns are predictable. That means you can prepare. Create a transition zone where they can decompress without pressure. Have calming sounds ready before they walk in. Delay questions about school. Feed them without asking what they want. Give them space to exist without masking.

Sound helps because it asks nothing. Press play. The frequencies fill the space. Your child's nervous system has something consistent and predictable to land on. They don't have to engage. They just have to receive it.

Some days the debt is too heavy and the meltdown happens anyway. That's okay. You haven't failed. Some days the school day is simply too much.

After six hours of holding it together, your child is home. And home is where they can finally let go.
