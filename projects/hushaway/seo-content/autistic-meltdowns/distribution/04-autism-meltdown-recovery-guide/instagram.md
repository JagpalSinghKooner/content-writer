Here's what nobody tells you about the hours after an autistic meltdown.

The visible meltdown is only part of the story. Often, it's not even the hardest part.

Parents call it the "meltdown hangover." That foggy, fragile state that can last for hours after the visible crisis ends.

Your child might seem spacey. Irritable at the smallest things. Unusually sensitive to sounds or touch. Quick to tip back over into another meltdown from almost nothing.

What's actually happening: A meltdown is a neurological event. Your child's nervous system went into overdrive. Their stress hormones spiked. Their thinking brain went offline.

You can't put a nervous system through that kind of storm and expect it to bounce back immediately.

## Visual Notes

**Carousel Design:**
- Colour palette: Soft, calming tones (gentle blues, muted purples, warm neutrals)
- Typography: Clean sans-serif, highly readable
- Cover slide: Bold statement text with negative space
- Content slides: One main point per slide with breathing room
- Avoid busy patterns or overwhelming visuals

**Reel B-roll Suggestions:**
- Close-up of child's hands (fidgeting, self-soothing)
- Parent sitting quietly nearby (presence without intrusion)
- Dimmed lighting transition
- Peaceful environment setup
- Text overlays with key phrases
- Avoid showing distressed children or active meltdowns
