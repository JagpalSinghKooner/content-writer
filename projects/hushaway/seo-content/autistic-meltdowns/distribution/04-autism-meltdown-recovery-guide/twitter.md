### Tweet 2
1. THE MELTDOWN HANGOVER

A meltdown isn't just an emotional outburst. It's a neurological event.

Your child's stress hormones spiked. Their thinking brain went offline.

You can't expect a nervous system to bounce back immediately.

### Tweet 3
2. PHYSICAL EXHAUSTION

Meltdowns are physically demanding.

Your child might be genuinely tired in a way that sleep alone won't fix. They might want to lie down. Move slowly.

This isn't them being dramatic. Their body genuinely needs rest.

### Tweet 4
3. EMOTIONAL FRAGILITY

The threshold for another meltdown drops dramatically after a recent one.

Things that would normally be fine can feel impossible.

The "buffer" that normally absorbs small frustrations is gone. It takes time to rebuild.

### Tweet 5
4. COGNITIVE FOG

Your child might seem confused. Forgetful. Unable to follow instructions that are normally easy.

This isn't defiance. The thinking brain is still coming back online after being offline during the meltdown.

Give it time.

### Tweet 6
5. SHAME & MEMORY GAPS

Many children don't fully remember what happened during a meltdown.

When you ask "why did you do that?" and they say "I don't know," they're often telling the truth.

Older children may feel deeply ashamed. This shame can make them withdraw.
