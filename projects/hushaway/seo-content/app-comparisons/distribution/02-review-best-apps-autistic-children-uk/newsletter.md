# Newsletter Snippet: Best Apps for Autistic Children UK 2026

## Hook

It's 11pm. Your autistic child is still awake, overwhelmed by a day that asked too much of their nervous system. You've downloaded three calming apps this week, and none of them worked.

## Body

Here's what most parents don't realise: most "autism-friendly" apps weren't designed for autistic processing. They were designed for neurotypical children and marketed to autism families later. The difference matters enormously.

Research shows up to 90% of autistic individuals experience sensory sensitivities. A voice that seems gentle to neurotypical ears might feel intrusive to your child. Background music meant to relax can become an unpredictable assault. And apps that require following instructions, making choices, or engaging actively? Those fight against autistic processing during the moments when your child needs help most.

The apps that actually work for autistic children respect how they process sound. They offer predictability instead of novelty. They require nothing from your child. They use sensory-safe audio design with consistent volume and smooth transitions. And they're built from the ground up for autism, not retrofitted later.

We reviewed the major calming apps through an autism lens: Moshi, Calm, Headspace, Smiling Mind, Sleep Wise. Some work for some children some of the time. But the apps designed specifically for autistic processing work differently. Press play, and that's it. No choices. No instructions. No sensory surprises.
