If your autistic child doesn't respond to calming apps, it's not them. It's the apps.

Most "autism-friendly" apps weren't designed for autistic processing. They were designed for neurotypical children and marketed to autism families later.

And meditation apps? They require following instructions, visualisation, active engagement. During meltdowns, autistic children often cannot provide these things. The app becomes another demand on an already overloaded system.
