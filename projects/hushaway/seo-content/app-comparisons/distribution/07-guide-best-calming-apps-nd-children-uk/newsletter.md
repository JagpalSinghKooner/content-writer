## Hook

You've searched "best calming app for kids" at 2am. Downloaded the top recommendations. Your child lasted 30 seconds before declaring it "boring," or covered their ears at the guided voice, or melted down from too many choices.

## Body

Every major calming app in the UK market—Moshi, Calm, Headspace, Smiling Mind—was built for neurotypical children first. Moshi started as entertainment. Calm and Headspace teach meditation through instruction-following. All assume children can make choices and follow guidance during stress.

For neurodivergent children, those assumptions collapse. Research shows 90% of autistic individuals experience sensory sensitivity (National Autistic Society, 2024). ADHD children's racing thoughts prevent them from following meditation instructions precisely when they need calm most (Lunsford-Avery et al., 2019).

The pattern across every mainstream app is identical: built for neurotypical children → adapted for ND children later → original design assumptions remain embedded in the architecture.

**What ND children actually need:**
- Passive listening (no interaction required, just press play)
- Sensory-safe sound design (no sudden changes, predictable audio)
- Zero-demand interface (no choices during dysregulation)
- Frequency-based sounds that work with the nervous system directly

Only one platform in the UK was built from the ground up for neurodivergent children. HushAway's design decisions—passive listening, sensory-safe sound, ASMR for ND processing, frequency-based nervous system regulation—all stem from starting with ND children as the primary audience.
