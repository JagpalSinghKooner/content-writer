## Text Post 3: What 90% of Parents Get Wrong

Most parents searching for calming apps make the same mistake.

They choose based on:
‚Üí Production values
‚Üí Celebrity narrators

‚Üí What worked for their friend's neurotypical child

**None of that matters if the design doesn't match your child's brain.**

Here's what actually determines whether a calming app works for neurodivergent children:

**1. Passive vs Active Listening**

Can your child benefit without following instructions?

Most apps require: "Breathe in for four counts." "Imagine a peaceful place." "Now press the button to continue."

During dysregulation, ND children can't do any of that. The cognitive resources needed to follow instructions go offline precisely when calm is needed most.

**2. Sensory Safety**

Does the sound design respect auditory sensitivities?

Research shows up to 90% of autistic individuals experience sensory sensitivity (National Autistic Society, 2024). Voices that seem gentle to neurotypical ears can feel intrusive to an autistic child. Background music with changing tempos keeps the nervous system on high alert.

**3. Zero-Demand Interaction**

How many decisions does the app require?

"Choose your mood." "Select a story." "Pick your character." Every decision is a demand on executive function. For a child already struggling to regulate, choices become barriers, not features.

The best calming apps for ND children minimise decisions. Ideally: no decisions at all. Just press play.
