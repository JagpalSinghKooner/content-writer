### 2/

Standard sleep advice says:

- Remove screens
- Create darkness
- Make it quiet

This works for neurotypical brains.

For ADHD brains? You've just removed every external focus.

Now their brain fills the vacuum with racing thoughts.

### 3/

ADHD brains don't have an automatic "off switch" for thoughts.

When external stimulation stops, internal stimulation ramps up.

The quieter the room, the louder the brain becomes.

### 4/

Melatonin helps your child feel sleepy.

But it doesn't quiet the racing brain.

Result: A child who is physically tired, mentally wired, and frustrated by the mismatch.

Melatonin addresses half the problem.

### 5/

The missing piece:

ADHD brains need INPUT to calm down, not just removal of input.

Read that again.

It's the opposite of what most sleep advice tells you.

### 6/

Research backs this up.

A 2024 meta-analysis found white and pink noise improved cognitive function in ADHD children.

The same sounds had the OPPOSITE effect on neurotypical children.

ADHD brains are genuinely wired differently.

### 7/

The leading theory: ADHD brains are under-aroused.

They're constantly seeking stimulation.

At bedtime, when external stimulation drops, the brain creates its own: racing thoughts, physical restlessness.
