# Twitter/X Distribution: Calming Sounds for ADHD Children

## Thread

**1/**
Not all calming sounds actually calm ADHD brains.

Some sounds that work for neurotypical children can increase alertness in ADHD kids. Others cause irritation.

Here's what the research says about which sounds genuinely help:

**2/**
Why does sound matter for ADHD at all?

An ADHD brain in a quiet room is like a hungry person in an empty kitchen.

It keeps hunting for stimulation. Checking every cupboard. Restless until it finds something.

**3/**
The right sound gives the brain what it's seeking.

Steady input that requires nothing back.

The searching stops. The brain settles.

**4/**
A 2024 meta-analysis looked at 335 children with ADHD.

White and pink noise improved focus and performance.

But here's the twist: those same sounds slightly REDUCED performance in neurotypical kids.

**5/**
Why the difference?

ADHD brains are under-aroused. They seek stimulation constantly.

The right amount of external sound brings arousal to the helpful range.

Neurotypical brains are already there, so noise pushes them past it.

**6/**
What works (research-backed):

WHITE NOISE: Most studied. Helps focus and attention. Best for inattentive-type ADHD.

PINK NOISE: Softer than white. Similar benefits. Easier on the ears at bedtime.

**7/**
What about brown noise?

Despite millions claiming it "cures" ADHD on social media...

The 2024 meta-analysis found: "No studies of brown noise were identified."

May still help. Just no peer-reviewed proof yet.

**8/**
What makes sound work for ADHD specifically:

- Passive (no interaction needed)
- Consistent (no sudden silence)
- Asks nothing from your child
- No instructions or guidance

The brain can just... be there.

**9/**
Warning signs the sound isn't right:

- Increased restlessness
- Covering ears
- Complaints it's annoying

Good signs:

- Body relaxes
- Breathing slows
- Less fidgeting
- They call it "boring" (perfect)

**10/**
One caution: volume matters.

Keep it moderate. You should hear your child without raising your voice.

Too loud through headphones = hearing damage risk.

**11/**
Finding what works takes trial and error.

But the research is clear: for many ADHD children, the right sound genuinely helps.

It gives the brain input that leads toward sleep instead of away from it.

**12/**
Best part?

Zero effort required.

No new habits. No behaviours to track. No instructions to follow.

Just press play.
