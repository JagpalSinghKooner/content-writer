## Caption

It's 9:47pm. Your child is wide awake.

You've done everything the books say. Star charts, earlier bedtimes, screens off at 6pm, warm milk, lavender spray, weighted blankets, routines so rigid you could set a clock by them.

And still. Every. Single. Night.

.

.

Here's what the parenting books don't tell you: your child isn't being defiant. They're not testing boundaries. They're not "just overtired." Their brain works differently. And that difference doesn't clock off when the lights go down.

**The ADHD brain won't switch off.** Racing thoughts don't respond to "just close your eyes." The brain is under-stimulated during the day, then comes alive at night when the world gets quiet. No structure. No distractions. Just every thought pushed aside since breakfast.

**The autistic brain craves predictability, but bedtime is chaos disguised as routine.** Leaving dinner, walking upstairs, running a bath, choosing pyjamas, brushing teeth, getting into bed, lights off, door closed. Seven transitions in under an hour. Each one a potential anxiety trigger.

And the paradox that confuses everyone: overtiredness triggers hyperactivity in many neurodivergent children. Their nervous system compensates for exhaustion by ramping up, not down. The exhausted child who can't stop bouncing? That's not defiance. That's biology.

If you've been blaming yourself, please stop. Right now. You're not failing because star charts don't work. You're parenting a child whose neurology requires different tools. Tools that weren't in the parenting books because those books weren't written for your child.

What works is understanding why bedtime is hard, then finding approaches that match your child's actual brain.
