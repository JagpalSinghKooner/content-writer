### Tweet 2
First: Generic "calming music" fails ND children for a reason

Classical music has tempo changes (alertness spikes)
Melodies engage pattern recognition (more mental work)
Playlists end (child wakes when sound stops)

Your child's brain needs sound DESIGNED for it, not adapted

### Tweet 3
**Sound Type 1: Ambient Soundscapes**

Rain, ocean, forest sounds. Zero cognitive demand.

Best for: Sensory-sensitive children, ages 0-5
When: Start 60-90 min before bed, continue all night
Volume: Background presence, not focal point

The brain can rest without needing to process anything

### Tweet 4
**Sound Type 2: ASMR**

Whispering, tapping, soft textures

Best for: Children who relax with gentle sounds
When: Active wind-down, 15-30 min before sleep

Important: Only ~20% of people experience ASMR. If your child doesn't respond, that's completely normal

### Tweet 5
**Sound Type 3: Frequencies & Binaural Beats**

Steady, unchanging tones

Best for: ADHD racing thoughts, ages 8+
When: Final settling phase, once in bed

Why it works: Gives the wandering brain somewhere to land without demanding engagement

### Tweet 6
**Sound Type 4: Gentle Narration**

Sleep stories, soft-spoken content (no instructions)

Best for: Racing thoughts, separation anxiety
When: Main settling activity

Avoid: Exciting plots or cliffhangers. Brain should relax, not wonder what happens next

### Tweet 7
Timing is where most parents get it wrong

Starting sound at lights-out asks it to do all the work in 5 minutes

Start 60-90 minutes BEFORE bed. Let it do gradual work across the entire wind-down

After a week, the brain starts settling automatically when it hears the evening soundscape
