You're not doing it wrong. The advice is incomplete.

Generic calming music fails neurodivergent children because it's designed for neurotypical brains. Tempo changes create alertness spikes. Melodies engage the brain in pattern recognition. Playlists end right when your child was starting to settle.

Sound CAN transform bedtime. But it needs to be the RIGHT sound, at the RIGHT time, used the RIGHT way.

Ambient soundscapes work for sensory-sensitive children (zero cognitive demand). ASMR helps sensory-seeking children (though only 20% of people respond to it). Frequencies give ADHD racing brains something steady to land on. Gentle narration provides presence for separation anxiety.

Timing matters as much as sound type. Start 60-90 minutes BEFORE bed, not at lights-out. Let sound do gradual work across the entire wind-down. Use the same sound every night for at least two weeks so the brain can build associations.
