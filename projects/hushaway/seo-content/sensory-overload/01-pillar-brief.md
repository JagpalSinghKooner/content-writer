# Pillar Brief: Sensory Overload

## 1. Strategic Context

**Pillar:** Sensory overload in children
**Priority:** High (Position 3 in production queue)
**Why now:** Core ND concern connecting ADHD, autism, and sensitive children. Search volume growing (2,500+ monthly UK). Product listicles dominate—no audio-first content exists.
**Proprietary advantage:** ASMR and solfeggio frequencies designed for sensory-safe listening. HushAway is the ONLY UK resource positioning sound AS THE SOLUTION for sensory overload, not just noise reduction.

**CRITICAL MARKET GAP IDENTIFIED:**

Perplexity research across 6 major UK competitors revealed:

> "The apparent gap in comprehensive guidance regarding audio and sound-based therapeutic interventions across these six platforms represents a significant limitation in current provision. While noise reduction through environmental modification constitutes a well-established and frequently recommended approach, **the positive therapeutic potential of carefully selected, appropriately timed sound input remains underemphasized.**"

**Translation:** Every competitor focuses on REDUCING sound (ear defenders, quiet spaces). NONE position sound as THE PRIMARY TOOL for calming sensory overload. HushAway can own this angle completely.

## 5. Angle Opportunities

Based on competitor gaps, these angles are available:

1. **"Sound AS THE Solution"** — Every competitor treats sound as one tool among many, or frames it as a problem to reduce. HushAway is the ONLY resource positioning audio-first calming. This is the primary pillar angle.

2. **"The Passive Listening Advantage"** — Competitors recommend tactile tools (weighted blankets, fidgets) that require motor control and attention. Sound requires NOTHING during peak overload. Zero-demand regulation.

3. **"The Right Sound Calms, The Wrong Sound Overwhelms"** — Address the paradox: sound-sensitive children CAN benefit from therapeutic sound. It's not about eliminating sound—it's about CONTROLLING it (predictable, safe frequencies vs unpredictable noise).

4. **"The Missing Sensory System in Sensory Diets"** — Traditional sensory diets focus on proprioception, vestibular, tactile. Auditory input is systematically missing. Position HushAway as filling this gap.

5. **"Recovery, Not Just Prevention"** — Most content addresses prevention or in-the-moment strategies. Position sound as THE recovery tool for post-overload exhaustion and sensory hangovers.

**Recommended starting angle:** **"Sound AS THE Solution"** (Angle #1). It's the most differentiated, directly addresses the competitor gap, and positions HushAway's entire product category as the answer. Use this as the primary pillar theme, with other angles supporting specific articles.
