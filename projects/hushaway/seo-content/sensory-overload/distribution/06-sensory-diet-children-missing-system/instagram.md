## Caption

Your child's sensory diet is missing the system that regulates fastest.

.
.
.

You've done everything the OT suggested. Heavy work. Swinging. Movement breaks. The whole checklist. And it helps. Sometimes. But not enough. Not consistently. Not when it really matters.

Here's what nobody told you: there's a sensory system missing from nearly every sensory diet. The one that regulates fastest. Auditory input.

Sound reaches your child's emotional centres in 12 milliseconds. That's faster than touch. Faster than movement. Faster than anything else you're doing. Yet most sensory diets treat sound as background noise, not as a regulation tool.

For exhausted parents, sound is the easiest sensory input to provide. No swing to set up. No heavy work to supervise. Press play. That's it. For depleted children after a long day of masking, sound requires nothing. No touch tolerance. No motor control. Just ears. Just presence.

If your child's sensory diet isn't working as well as you'd hoped, try adding auditory input. Morning routine. After school. Homework time. Transitions. Bedtime. Give the auditory system the attention it deserves.
