Here's the thing everyone misses: the hours after sensory overload matter just as much as the meltdown itself.

After the peak passes, the nervous system doesn't just snap back to normal. Think of it like a storm. The rain has stopped, but the ground is still flooded. Your child's nervous system is still doing the work of recovery.

This is the sensory hangover. And if you've ever watched your child struggle for hours after a meltdown, wondering why they can't just "snap out of it," this is why.

What helps isn't more talking, processing, or lessons. What helps is reducing demands to almost nothing. Creating space for the nervous system to recover without having to process anything new.

Sound becomes a recovery tool here that nothing else can match. Press play on something gentle. No questions. No requests. Just something soft for their nervous system to land on whilst it repairs.

Recovery is where the nervous system actually heals. Skip it, rush it, or ignore it, and you're setting up the next overload. Support it properly, and you're breaking the cycle.

The quiet matters as much as the crisis. Treat it that way.
