The weighted blanket worked twice. Then your child refused to go near it.

You've got a drawer full of sensory tools. And during peak overload? None of them work.

Here's what nobody tells you: most sensory tools work brilliantly for prevention. But during crisis, they require abilities that overload takes away.

Motor control disappears. Touch tolerance drops. Cognitive capacity vanishes. The tools you have need the very abilities that crisis steals.

Sound is different. It requires nothing from your child. No motor control. No touch tolerance. No choices. Just press play.

Sound bypasses every system that goes offline during overload. It works from a distance. You can press play and step back, giving them the space they need whilst still doing something to help.

Don't throw out the sensory drawer. Weighted blankets, fidgets, and movement have their place. Use them for prevention and mild stress.

But during peak overload, when your child can't engage with anything else? Sound is the tool that works when everything else fails.

The drawer isn't complete without it.
