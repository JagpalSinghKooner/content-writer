# Newsletter Snippet: Sensory Overload in Children - The Complete Guide

## Hook

You've tried the weighted blanket. It got thrown across the room. The fidget spinners are somewhere under the sofa. The ear defenders lasted approximately forty-five seconds. And still your child is in sensory overload.

## Body

Here's what nobody tells you: sound isn't the enemy. It's the solution.

Every piece of sensory advice focuses on reducing input. Quieten the environment. Dim the lights. Block out noise. And while those strategies help with prevention, they don't work during crisis.

During crisis, your child needs something different. Something that requires nothing from them when they have nothing left to give.

Sound is that tool. It's the only sensory input that's completely passive. No touching. No coordinating. No effort. Just press play. Step back. Let it work.

This isn't about all sound. It's about the right sound. Therapeutic, predictable frequencies that give the overwhelmed nervous system something safe to anchor to.

We've written a complete guide covering everything UK parents need to know about sensory overload in children:

• What it actually is (neurological, not behavioural)
• How to recognise it before it hits crisis point
• Why sound works when weighted blankets, fidgets, and ear defenders fail
• The sound sensitivity paradox (why sound-sensitive children still benefit from therapeutic sound)
• After-school meltdowns and cumulative overload
• The recovery phase no one mentions
• Building sound into daily regulation, not just crisis response

Because one quiet moment can change a whole day for a child.
