## Consistency Check

**All angles share:** Sound as a regulation tool (not just entertainment or background noise). Passive listening as the zero-demand solution. Therapeutic sound (ASMR, frequencies) differentiated from overwhelming noise.

**Primary angle establishes:** The core contrarian position—sound IS the solution, not the problem. Challenges the market's "reduce all sound" narrative.

**Supporting angles extend via:**
- **Article 1 (Understanding):** Explains WHY sound regulates (neurological basis, auditory system's direct connection to emotion centres)

- **Article 3 (After-School):** WHEN to use sound (first intervention after school). Temporal positioning.
- **Article 4 (Sensory Tools):** Sound vs tactile tools (comparative positioning). Establishes sound's unique advantage during crisis.
- **Article 5 (Sound Sensitivity):** Addresses objection (therapeutic sound ≠ triggering noise). Risk reversal.
- **Article 6 (Sensory Diet):** Sound as daily regulation (not just crisis). Expands use case from reactive to proactive.

The distinction is the volume, predictability, and neurological design. Frame as: "Not all sound is created equal—here's how to tell the difference."
