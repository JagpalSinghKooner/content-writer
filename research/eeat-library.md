## Sleep Problems in ADHD Children

### Core Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Year | Link | How to Cite (Warm) |
|-----------|--------|------|------|-------------------|
| 25-55% prevalence of sleep disturbances in ADHD | Multiple studies, PMC Review | 2024 | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12620782/) | "Research suggests that between a quarter and over half of children with ADHD experience significant sleep difficulties." |
| 62% of ADHD children have moderate or severe sleep problems | Australian study | Recent | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12620782/) | "An Australian study found that over 60% of children with ADHD have moderate or severe sleep problems." |
| 35-70% prevalence range (varies by subtype, age, medication) | Multiple studies | Various | [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2022.919572/full) | "Studies show that between 35% and 70% of children with ADHD struggle with sleep, depending on factors like age and whether they take medication." |
| 82% of ADHD children exceed threshold for paediatric sleep disorder | Clinical study | Recent | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10952554/) | "One clinical study found that over 80% of children with ADHD met the criteria for a sleep disorder." |
| 74.6% incidence of co-morbid sleep disorders | Research study | Various | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9354977/) | "Research shows that around three-quarters of children with ADHD have a co-existing sleep difficulty." |
| 8x increased risk of sleep disorder diagnosis vs non-ADHD | Swedish nationwide registry (N=6.4M) | 2023 | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10577710/) | "A large Swedish study found that individuals with ADHD are eight times more likely to be diagnosed with a sleep disorder." |

### Key Points for Articles

**Why ADHD affects sleep:**
- Delayed sleep onset (trouble falling asleep)
- Frequent nighttime awakenings
- Morning fatigue and difficulty waking
- Excessive daytime sleepiness

**Bidirectional relationship:**
- Sleep deprivation increases inattention and emotional dysregulation
- Evening hyperactivity and impulsivity make falling asleep harder

**Citation example:**
> "If bedtime feels like a nightly battle, you are not imagining it. Research shows that between 35% and 70% of children with ADHD struggle with sleep. This is not a discipline issue; it is how their brains work."

## Caregiver Burnout

### Core Statistics

| Statistic | Source | Year | Link | How to Cite (Warm) |
|-----------|--------|------|------|-------------------|
| 65% of parents of neurodivergent children at higher risk for burnout | Cleo Family Health Index | 2024 | [Cleo Report](https://hicleo.com/resource/caregiving-deep-dive-65-of-parents-of-neurodivergent-children-at-higher-risk-for-burnout/) | "According to the Cleo Family Health Index, 65% of parents of neurodivergent children are at higher risk for burnout." |
| 97% increase in burnout risk vs average parents (33%) | Cleo Family Health Index | 2024 | Same as above | "Parents of neurodivergent children face nearly double the burnout risk of other parents." |
| Parents of neurodivergent children are the highest-risk group for burnout | Cleo Family Health Index | 2024 | Same as above | "Research identifies parents of neurodivergent children as the highest-risk group for caregiver burnout." |
| Parents of ASD children have higher stress, depression, and burnout than parents of neurotypical children | Multiple studies | Various | [PMC Editorial](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11496123/) | "Studies consistently show that parents of autistic children experience higher levels of stress and burnout." |

### Burnout Warning Signs (From Research)

Four key features distinguish parental burnout:
1. Extreme emotional and physical exhaustion related to parenting
2. Loss of fulfillment from parenting role
3. Emotional distancing from children (unconscious protective measure)
4. Contrast to how they previously felt about being a parent

**Citation example:**
> "Burnout is not just tiredness. Researchers describe it as extreme exhaustion, losing the joy in parenting, and finding yourself emotionally withdrawing from your children. If this sounds familiar, you are not failing; your body is protecting itself."

## Citation Format Guide

### Warm Citation Style (HushAway®)

**DO:**
- "Research shows that..." + finding
- "A 2024 study found that..."
- "According to the [Organisation Name]..."
- "Evidence suggests that..."

**DON'T:**
- "According to Smith et al. (2023) in the Journal of..."
- "A meta-analysis conducted by researchers at..."
- "The literature demonstrates that..."

### Example Transformations

**Academic style (don't use):**
> "Lu et al. (2024) found that parents of children with ADHD were more than four times more likely to be depressed (OR=4.2, p<0.001)."

**HushAway® warm style (use this):**
> "Research from 2024 found that parents of children with ADHD are more than four times more likely to experience depression. If you're struggling, please know this is not a personal failing."
