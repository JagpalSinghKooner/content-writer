## Section 5: People Also Ask Questions

### PAA Questions (10)

1. **Q:** Do focus apps really help children with ADHD?
   **Answer Approach:** Honest: some do, for specific things. 2025 RCT showed gamified apps improved attention (effect sizes 0.70-0.85). But most assume regulation. If child is dysregulated, focus apps won't help until that's addressed.

2. **Q:** What is the best app to help an ADHD child focus on homework?
   **Answer Approach:** Depends on the barrier. Task initiation? Try Todoist for breakdown. Sustained focus? Try Focus Keeper (Pomodoro). But if they can't START, try HushAway first to regulate.

3. **Q:** Does the Pomodoro technique work for ADHD children?
   **Answer Approach:** Yes, research supports it. 25-min intervals align with ADHD attention capacity. But customise: younger children may need 15-min intervals. Apps like Focus Keeper make it easy.

4. **Q:** Are focus apps safe or will they increase screen time?
   **Answer Approach:** Valid concern. Distinguish active engagement (games, scrolling) from tool use (timer, checklist). Focus apps = brief, purposeful screen use. HushAway = can use without looking at screen at all.

5. **Q:** How do I get my child to actually use a focus app?
   **Answer Approach:** Involve them in choosing. Build into existing routines. Start with ONE app. Don't expect independent use - support the habit. Celebrate app use itself, not just task completion.

6. **Q:** Which focus app is best for different types of ADHD?
   **Answer Approach:** Inattentive: task breakdown apps (Todoist), timers (Focus Keeper). Hyperactive-impulsive: movement breaks, gamified elements. Combined: flexibility needed. But ALL types benefit from regulation first.

7. **Q:** Should I use a free focus app or pay for one?
   **Answer Approach:** Free often means ads and data collection. 95% of free kids apps have ads. Paid apps (£3-10/month) typically ad-free, privacy-respecting. HushAway's Sound Sanctuary is free forever, no ads.

8. **Q:** Can focus apps replace medication or therapy?
   **Answer Approach:** No. Apps are tools, not treatments. Research shows apps work best as adjuncts to evidence-based care. Use apps to reduce daily friction while pursuing proper assessment and support.

9. **Q:** How do I choose between all the different focus apps?
   **Answer Approach:** Start with the specific problem: task initiation, sustained focus, time blindness, or regulation. Match app to problem. If overwhelmed or dysregulated, start with passive support (HushAway).

10. **Q:** What features matter most in an ADHD focus app?
    **Answer Approach:** Visual time representation (not just numbers), persistent reminders, task breakdown, simple interface (2-3 clicks to use), customisation. Avoid complexity - it adds cognitive load.
