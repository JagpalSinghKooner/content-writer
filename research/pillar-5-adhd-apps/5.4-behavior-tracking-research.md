### PAA Questions (10 - exceeds minimum)

1. **Q:** Is using a behaviour tracking app actually helping, or is it just making me obsess over my child's behaviour?
   **Answer Approach:** Validate the concern. Research shows parents become compulsive checkers. Discuss when tracking helps vs harms. HushAway alternative: passive support without data obsession.

2. **Q:** What behaviours should I actually be tracking, and how do I record them?
   **Answer Approach:** Introduce ABC method simply. Emphasise quality over quantity. Warn against tracking everything. Suggest focus on one or two concerns max.

3. **Q:** How do I know if my child's behaviour is ADHD, tiredness, or normal for their age?
   **Answer Approach:** This is the hardest question. Acknowledge the impossibility of always knowing. NICE guidance: symptoms must persist 6+ months, multiple settings. Don't pathologise everything.

4. **Q:** How do I know if I'm seeing a real pattern or just noticing what I expect?
   **Answer Approach:** Address confirmation bias directly. Suggest involving another observer. Mention that parent-teacher tracking agreement is often weak, which is normal and informative.

5. **Q:** Will behaviour tracking actually help my child, or am I just collecting data?
   **Answer Approach:** Evidence shows tracking helps IF it leads to action. Data without strategy is just stress. HushAway positioning: we skip the data and go straight to calming support.

6. **Q:** If my child knows I'm tracking their behaviour, will they feel ashamed?
   **Answer Approach:** Critical concern. Research shows children (especially ADHD) get disproportionate negative points. Frame tracking as understanding, involve child where appropriate, or use invisible support.

7. **Q:** How should home tracking relate to school behaviour tracking?
   **Answer Approach:** Address Class Charts concerns. Children behave differently at school vs home (masking). Discrepancies are informative, not failures. Advocate for reasonable adjustments.

8. **Q:** Why does checking the behaviour app make me so anxious?
   **Answer Approach:** Validate fully. Research shows parents check compulsively, experience anxiety spikes at negative points, feel helpless. This is not a personal failing. Offer boundaries.

9. **Q:** How do I discuss tracked behaviour with my child without blame?
   **Answer Approach:** Focus on patterns, not problems. "I noticed..." language. Involve child in solutions. Avoid shame. Some children benefit from not knowing at all.

10. **Q:** The app shows patterns, but what do I actually DO with that information?
    **Answer Approach:** Biggest gap in current content. Tracking without strategy is pointless. Offer specific interventions: if afternoon meltdowns, try snack + sensory break. HushAway positioning: Sound Sanctuary for transition support.
