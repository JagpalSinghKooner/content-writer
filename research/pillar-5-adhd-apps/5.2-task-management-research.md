### PAA Questions (9 discovered)

1. **Q:** Do reward systems actually work for kids with ADHD?
   **Answer Approach:** Yes, backed by neuroscience - ADHD brains need external rewards to compensate for reduced internal reward sensitivity. Warm, not clinical.

2. **Q:** How do I use visual schedules without it becoming nagging?
   **Answer Approach:** The schedule replaces the nagging - child checks schedule independently. Implementation tips.

3. **Q:** Will my child become dependent on rewards?
   **Answer Approach:** Address guilt, explain this is scaffolding during development, gradual fading over time.

4. **Q:** Which chore app actually works for kids with ADHD?
   **Answer Approach:** Depends on the child - match app features to specific challenges. Give criteria not just list.

5. **Q:** How can I help my child keep track of homework without micromanaging?
   **Answer Approach:** Collaborative systems, weekly check-ins, app as external brain not surveillance tool.

6. **Q:** Will task tracking apps make my child's anxiety worse?
   **Answer Approach:** Valid concern for 30-50% with co-occurring anxiety. Choose progress-focused apps, not performance-tracking.

7. **Q:** Will my child actually build a habit using an app, or is it just novelty?
   **Answer Approach:** Honest about 60-90 day requirement, novelty effect, and importance of parental scaffolding.

8. **Q:** How do visual timers help with time blindness?
   **Answer Approach:** Make abstract time concrete and visible. Explain time blindness warmly.

9. **Q:** Are there any good free ADHD task management apps?
   **Answer Approach:** Yes - Todoist free, physical alternatives, but address trade-offs honestly.
