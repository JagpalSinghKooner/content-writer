# Content Atomizer Skill

## The Core Job

Transform source content into **platform-native assets** that:
- Match each platform's algorithm signals
- Use format-specific best practices
- Include hooks proven to stop the scroll
- Feel native, not repurposed

## Platform Playbooks

### LinkedIn

**Algorithm Signals (December 2025):**
- **Dwell time** â€” How long people spend reading (still #1)
- **Topic authority** â€” Consistent niche posting builds algorithmic trust
- **Golden hour** â€” First 60 minutes determines reach to 2nd/3rd-degree connections
- **Relevance over recency** â€” Mid-2025 update shows older posts (2-3 weeks) if highly relevant
- **Authentic engagement** â€” AI detects engagement pods via comment velocity and patterns
- **Native content** â€” Posts without links get significantly more reach

**2025 Reality Check:** Organic views down ~50%, engagement down ~25% as LinkedIn prioritizes quality over quantity. Post 2-3x/week max, not daily.

**Optimal Specs:**

- **Carousel:** 5-10 slides, 1080x1350px. Highest dwell time, save-worthy
- **Text Post:** 1,200-1,500 chars, 3-line hook. Depth over frequency
- **Document:** PDF, 10-15 pages max. Good for frameworks
- **Video:** 30-90 seconds, captions required. Lower reach than text/carousels

### YouTube

**Algorithm Signals (December 2025):**
- **Click-through rate (CTR)** â€” How often people click when shown your video
- **Average view duration & % watched** â€” Raw watch time AND completion percentage
- **Session impact** â€” Does your video keep people on YouTube longer?
- **Viewer satisfaction** â€” YouTube now uses surveys + behavior to estimate quality
- **Negative feedback** â€” "Not interested," skips, very low retention hurt you
- **Topical authority** â€” Channels focused on clear topics get recommended more

**2025 Changes:** AI-driven hyper-personalization (device, time of day, habits). Older evergreen videos get revived when topics trend again. Stronger emphasis on authority, depth, and "entity-rich" content (aligned with Google Search updates).

**Optimal Specs:**

- **Shorts:** 10-35 sec (discovery), up to 60 sec. Highest reach, lower depth
- **Long-form:** 8-12 minutes (sweet spot). Best for monetisation + depth
- **Thumbnail:** 1280x720px. CTR target: 4-10% (Home), 6-12% strong

### Step 1: Extract

From your source content, pull out:

```

[One sentence that captures the main point]

2. [Point + brief explanation]
```

### Step 2: Map to Platforms

- **Core insight** â†’ All platforms â†’ Single posts, hooks
- **Supporting points (together)** â†’ LinkedIn, Twitter â†’ Carousel, thread
- **Individual points** â†’ All platforms â†’ Single posts
- **Stories** â†’ Instagram, TikTok â†’ Reels, Stories
- **Data points** â†’ LinkedIn, Twitter â†’ Image posts, carousels
- **Quotable lines** â†’ Twitter, Instagram â†’ Quote graphics
- **Contrarian takes** â†’ Twitter, TikTok â†’ Single tweets, video hooks

### Step 3: Transform

For each platform, apply:

1. **Format** â€” Use the templates above
2. **Hook** â€” Platform-specific hook formula
3. **Length** â€” Match platform norms
4. **CTA** â€” Platform-appropriate action
5. **Voice** â€” Adjust formality (LinkedIn > Instagram > TikTok)

### Step 4: Sequence

**Optimal posting sequence:**

1. **LinkedIn carousel** â€” Day 1 (longest shelf life)
2. **Twitter thread** â€” Day 1-2 (good for discussion)
3. **Instagram carousel** â€” Day 2-3 (repurpose LinkedIn design)
4. **TikTok/Reel** â€” Day 3-4 (needs video production)
5. **YouTube Short** â€” Day 4-5 (can repurpose TikTok)
6. **Single posts** â€” Ongoing (extract individual points)

## Anti-Patterns

- **No cross-posting** â€” Adapt per platform; copy-paste performs 40-60% worse
- **Different hooks per platform** â€” LinkedIn hooks â‰  TikTok hooks; adjust energy and format
- **Use native features** â€” Captions on video, visual-first on Instagram, no hashtags on LinkedIn carousels
- **Stagger posting** â€” Spread across days/weeks so each piece gets room to perform
- **Always include a CTA** â€” Platform-appropriate next action on every piece

## Platform Voice Adjustments

The same insight needs different energy per platform:

- **LinkedIn** â€” Professional, thoughtful: "After 10 years in marketing, I've learned that simplicity beats complexity. Here's why:"
- **Twitter** â€” Punchy, direct: "Hot take: Simple marketing > 'sophisticated' marketing. Every time."
- **Instagram** â€” Visual, inspirational: [Image with text: "Simple > Sophisticated" + story in caption]
- **TikTok** â€” Casual, energetic: "Y'all I need to talk about why everyone's overcomplicating their marketing..."
- **YouTube** â€” Conversational, thorough: "If you've been in marketing for any length of time, you've probably noticed something..."

## Quick Reference: Platform Specs (December 2025)

- **LinkedIn:** 1,200-1,500 chars, carousel, hook in first 3 lines, top signal: dwell time + topic authority
- **Twitter/X:** <100 chars (single), thread (8-15), hook in first tweet, top signal: replies + early engagement
- **Instagram:** 6-10 slides, carousel, hook on first slide, top signal: DM shares ("sends per reach")
- **TikTok:** 30-60 sec (if retention high), short video, hook in first 3 seconds, top signal: completion + niche alignment
- **YouTube Shorts:** 10-35 sec, vertical video, hook in first 2 seconds, top signal: completion rate
- **YouTube Long:** 8-12 min, horizontal, hook in first 30 seconds, top signal: satisfaction + session time

## The Test

Good atomization means:

1. **Each piece stands alone** â€” Makes sense without the source
2. **Each piece feels native** â€” Doesn't feel "repurposed"
3. **Hooks match the platform** â€” Right energy, right format
4. **Value is front-loaded** â€” Best stuff first
5. **CTAs are appropriate** â€” Platform-native actions
6. **Quality over quantity** â€” 5 great pieces > 15 mediocre ones
